--- layout: quote title: "Codebase as infrastructure" date: 2026-04-26 lang: en ref: codebase-infrastructure tags: [Agentic AI, Software Architecture, Developer Role] quote: "Your codebase has now become infrastructure and as such you have to design it in a way so that it is also legible for the agent and it can make the most of it." source_author: "Cristina Poncela Cubeiro" source_title: "The Friction is Your Judgment — Earendil" source_url: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Zcw_sVF6hU" source_label: "Watch on YouTube" source_embed: '' --- Days ago a colleague and friend shared this video with me. In it, Armin Ronacher — the creator of Flask, someone I highly respect after having used Flask for many years — and his colleague Cristina describe a framework for working with agentic coding that reframed things for me. We as developers are not building tools for ourselves to ease our dev process anymore. We're building the infrastructure — the guidelines, the rails, the boundaries — for the agents that will act on our behalf. That's the shift. The codebase isn't just something you read and the machine executes. It's now the environment where a third actor lives and operates. We've put down the hammer and the pickaxe. Now we have to wear the white hat. The architect's hat. We're at a meta-level: designing the space, setting the constraints, making the structure legible — not just for humans, but for the agents navigating it autonomously.
